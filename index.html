Halloween Harvest

We were all set on Thursday morn,
To get to the farm and harvest some corn
With luggage in hand and eager to go,
The flight was delayed because of snow.
In Minneapolis we finally arrived
Late and tired, but glad to survive
Do not drive, the radio warned
But we had to go get that corn.
We have a buick, who needs a truck
Drive through the storm, we won’t get stuck!
the going got tough and we had to choose
Should we keep on driving or stop to snooze?
The Happy Chef looked good to us all
We knew in the morning we’d make the right call.
It’s a new day and mom’s at the wheel
Southward bound, she’s full of zeal.
Passing snowplows and bucking those drifts
Until that burning smell we started to sniff.
We stopped at Lake Crystal to decide what to do,
We soon met the doctor and the Skipper, too.
Let’s get in the car and go to my house
Wait a minute, Weldon, where’s your spouse?!
We just needed shelter, we weren’t choosy,
But we were pleasantly surprised to find a jacuzzi!
Two days later and we’re on our way
Let’s get to the farm for at least one day
Four hours later and we’re close to the place,
Poor mom had to walk with wind in her face.
A good night’s sleep and then met Bruce
We didn’t stay long, had to get home for goose!
That corn still stands, but what can we say?
We’ll have to come back and get it in May!
October 31 ~ November 3, 1991
Pam & Ron (Gilligan & Mary Ann)
